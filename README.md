## **Overview (概要)**

This research artifact, produced by the **GhostDrift Mathematical Institute**, challenges the traditional interpretation of Ludwig Wittgenstein's "silence" as a termination of thought. Instead, it redefines the linguistic threshold as the **"Dynamics of Responsibility"**—a critical flashpoint where ethical state transitions occur.

Amidst the "crisis of meaning" accelerated by generative AI, this paper posits that the "unsayable" is not a mystical void, but a structural component necessary for the preservation of human inquiry and social systems.

### **2\. The Tri-phasic Model of Silence**

The paper models three distinct socio-ethical manifestations at the threshold:

* **State 1: Nullification (無効化)**  
  * Meaning is reduced to predictable noise (System Collapse).  
* **Threshold: Silence (沈黙)**  
  * The activation point.  
* **State 2: Infinite Inquiry (無限の問い)**  
  * The leap into questioning (Ethical Survival).  
* **State 3: Return as Death / Existential Rupture (責任の破断)**  
  * Defined as a **responsibility rupture**: the hinge is destroyed, inquiry cannot regenerate, and the system returns with irreversible loss of meaning (institutional or existential).

### **3\. Hinge Epistemology as Design Constraint**

Proposing that Wittgenstein's "Hinges" (certainties beyond doubt) must be implemented not just as philosophical concepts, but as **"Existential Design Constraints"** in AI governance to prevent the collapse of meaning.
